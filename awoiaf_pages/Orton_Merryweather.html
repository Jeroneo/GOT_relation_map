Orton Merryweather is the Lord of Longtable and the head of House Merryweather.

Appearance and Character

Orton has a large, lumpy and bulbous nose with messy orange-red hair. He is always courteous[2] and can be sycophantic.[2][3]

History

Orton is the grandson of Lord Owen Merryweather,[4] a Hand of the King to Aerys II Targaryen who was exiled from Westeros for failing to contain Robert's Rebellion.[5] Orton went into exile with his grandfather where he found a Myrish bride, Taena.[6][1]

During his reign,[N 1] King Robert I Baratheon was persuaded by Orton to restore the lands, castle and titles stripped by Aerys to the Merryweathers. Robert did not grant them all of their lands back, however, and also emptied the family treasury, leaving Orton a less wealthy and powerful lord than his grandfather had been.[7][1][8][9]

Recent Events

A Storm of Swords

Orton is among the lords who travel to King's Landing when Margaery Tyrell is promised to King Joffrey I Baratheon. He brings his wife, Lady Taena of Myr.[10]

Lord Merryweather dances with Sansa Stark at her wedding feast.[11]

Joffrey chokes to death during his royal wedding.[12] Taena falsely testifies that she saw Tyrion Lannister drop something into the wedding chalice during the feast.[13]

A Feast for Crows

Queen Regent Cersei Lannister names Lord Orton to the small council of King Tommen I Baratheon as justiciar. In their first session, he counsels making cause with the ironborn to make up for the Iron Throne's lack of sea power after the Battle of the Blackwater, and granting them part of the north despite House Bolton's dominion there. Orton further suggests turning on the ironmen if the Boltons defeat Stannis Baratheon, though ultimately Cersei elects to build her own fleet.[2]

Ser Jaime Lannister thinks that Orton convinced Cersei to name Ser Bonifer Hasty as castellan of Harrenhal.[14]

Orton's wife Taena becomes Cersei's confidant and often sleeps in the queen's bed.[3]

Taena conspires with Cersei against Queen Margaery, the new bride of the regent's son, King Tommen. When Cersei decides to have the Merryweathers sup with her in the middle of the night, a pale-faced Orton drinks heavily and steals glances at the singer performing for them, the Blue Bard, whom Cersei is planning to frame. Cersei raises Orton to be the new Hand of the King after she demotes Ser Harys Swyft to lord treasurer. Cersei falsely accuses the Blue Bard of having slept with Margaery, and she orders Orton to have the singer taken to the dungeons.[4]

Lord Merryweather worries what the smallfolk will think after Margaery is imprisoned by the Faith of the Seven. After Cersei is herself imprisoned by the Faith, Lord Orton resigns his position and flees back to Longtable with Taena.[15]
