Donnel Waynwood is a knight from House Waynwood and the second son of Lady Anya Waynwood, Lady of Ironoaks.[2]

His squire is Sandor Frey, a kinsman of some relation.[2]

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Donnel is portrayed by Alisdair Simpson.

Appearance and Character

A stocky youth in his early twenties, Ser Donnel has a wide nose and a shock of thick brown hair. Lady Catelyn Stark describes him as earnest and homely.[1]

Like most Waynwoods, Donnel was always one for ceremony and tradition. He speaks with the solemn courtesy of a youth.[1]

Recent Events

A Game of Thrones

After the death of Lord Jon Arryn, the mountain clans of the Vale grew bolder. Ser Donnel desires to lead a hundred men to root out the mountain clans, but Lady Lysa Arryn forbids it, wanting to keep the swords of the Vale close to home.[1]

Ser Donnel leads the sortie from the Bloody Gate that saves Lady Catelyn Stark's party from the clans in the Mountains of the Moon. After escorting her party to the Bloody Gate, Donnel allows the wounded to recuperate within the battlements. He also sends ravens to the Eyrie and Gates of the Moon, informing Lady Arryn of her sister's arrival.[1]

Lady Anya Waynwood is present in the High Hall of the Eyrie, surrounded by her sons, when Tyrion Lannister demands a trial by battle to prove his innocence of the claims against him.[5]

A Clash of Kings

Donnel has replaced Ser Brynden Tully as the new Knight of the Gate at the Bloody Gate after Brynden's resignation.[2][6]

A Feast for Crows

When Lord Petyr Baelish teaches her some Arryn genealogy and their relation to the Waynwoods, Alayne Stone recalls that Donnel serves as the Knight of the Gate.[6][7][8]
