Tobho Mott is a Qohorik master armorer in King's Landing. His shop is at the top of the Street of Steel in the capital.[1]

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Tobho is portrayed by Andrew Wilde.[3]

Appearance and Character

See also: Images of Tobho Mott

He owns a black velvet coat which has silver hammers embroidered on the sleeves. A large sapphire hangs on a heavy silver chain about his neck.[1]

Tobho is stubborn and loyal to his staff. He is one of the few armorers who can work Valyrian steel,[4] and he claims to be the only armorer in King's Landing who can add tint to the metals in a suit of armor without using paint.[1]

History

Tobho learned to smith in Qohor.[1]

Ser Loras Tyrell and Lord Renly Baratheon are among the high lords who have purchased armor from Tobho.[1]

To intimidate his opponents and their horses in tourneys, Thoros of Myr dips his sword of cheap steel in wildfire and sets it ablaze. Since the wildfire ruins the blade, Thoros buys a new one from Tobho after each competition, with the two of them always arguing about the price.[5] Tobho told Gendry, one of his apprentices, that Thoros could outdrink King Robert I Baratheon, and the armorer taught Gendry to be polite toward highborn girls.[6]

Recent Events

A Game of Thrones

Lord Eddard Stark visits Tobho's shop and sees Gendry, by Ryan Barger © Fantasy Flight Games

Lord Eddard Stark, Hand of the King, investigates the death of his predecessor, Lord Jon Arryn. Jory Cassel, Eddard's captain, learns from a potboy that Jon had been considering commissioning plate wrought in pale silver with a blue jasper falcon and a mother-of-pearl moon on the breast.

Visiting Tobho's shop, Eddard asks the master if he made a falcon helm for Lord Arryn. Tobho explains that Jon and Lord Stannis Baratheon did not patronize his work, but instead were interested in one of his blacksmiths. Tobho introduces Ned to Gendry, one of King Robert I Baratheon's unacknowledged bastards who has been working for him as an apprentice. While protective of Gendry's identity, Tobho reveals that the boy's apprentice fee was paid by a stout, brown-bearded man who wore a hooded velvet cloak.[1]

A Clash of Kings

Gendry reveals to Arya Stark that he was ordered to join the Night's Watch by Master Mott.[7] According to a semi-canon source, Lord Varys had Tobho send Gendry to Yoren to protect the boy after Ned's downfall.[8][9]

A Storm of Swords

Gendry tells Arya that Tobho had given him good steel so the youth could forge his first longsword, but Gendry was taken away by Yoren before he could do so.[5]

At Lord Tywin Lannister's request, Tobho melts the late Lord Stark's Valyrian steel sword, Ice, to forge two new swords. Showing his work to Tywin in the Tower of the Hand, the armorer admits that he tried to color the swords the crimson of House Lannister. The steel darkened to grey and red patterns despite his spells, however.[4] One sword is given to King Joffrey I Baratheon, who names it Widow's Wail.[10] The other is given to Ser Jaime Lannister, who names it Oathkeeper when he gives it in turn to Brienne of Tarth.[11]
