Walter Whent was the Lord of Harrenhal and the head of House Whent during the reign of King Aerys II Targaryen.[1][2]

According to a semi-canon source, Walter was married to Shella Whent.[N 1] According to A Game of Thrones and A Clash of Kings, Shella is also from House Whent.[3][4]

Character

According to Ser Barristan Selmy, Walter was as open handed as he was rich.[5]

History

In 281 AC, the year of the false spring, Lord Whent hosted the great tourney at Harrenhal to honor his maiden daughter.[6][2] Ser Barristan Selmy recalls that the tourney was announced by Walter shortly after he was visited by his brother, Ser Oswell Whent of the Kingsguard.[7] Most people believed that Walter was attempting to outdo the Anniversary Tourney hosted by Lord Tywin Lannister in 272 AC, but some believed that a "shadow host"—possibly Prince Rhaegar Targaryen—provided the funding for Walter's extravagant prizes.[2]

Lord Whent's daughter reigned as queen of love and beauty when the tourney began. Her four brothers and her uncle, Oswell, swore to defend her crown, but Prince Rhaegar won the joust and named Lyanna Stark the new queen of love and beauty.[8][5] King Aerys II Targaryen, Rhaegar's father, unexpectedly attended the tourney and was suspicious of Walter.[2]

Ser Jorah Mormont defeated a Lord Whent in the tourney at Lannisport in 289 AC; it is unknown if it was Walter or one of his descendants.[9]

Recent Events

A Dance with Dragons

Ser Barristan Selmy remembers Walter as "Old Lord Whent".[7]

Their relation to each other, besides their marriage, is as of yet unknown. Until any confirmation is given, a clearer family tree showing all relations of the Whents cannot be depicted.
