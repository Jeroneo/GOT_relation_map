Hallyne, also known as Hallyne the Pyromancer, is the head of the Alchemists' Guild. He is a third generation alchemist.[2]

In the television adaptation Game of Thrones, Hallyne is portrayed by Roy Dotrice.

Appearance

See also: Images of Hallyne

Hallyne is a pallid man with soft damp hands.[2] He dresses in striped black-and-scarlet robes trimmed with patchy and moth-eaten sable.[2]

History

Hallyne's family has been involved in the Alchemists' Guild for three generations. His father was wisdom in the days of King Aerys II Targaryen and his grandfather in those of Jaehaerys II Targaryen.[3]

An acolyte during the reign of Aerys II,[3] Hallyne was trained by Wisdom Pollitor.[3] He was likely unaware of the wildfire plot being planned by Rossart and the other Wisdoms, as according to Ser Jaime Lannister the alchemists did all the work in secret and did not trust their own apprentices with the knowledge.[4]

Recent Events

A Clash of Kings

Hallyne is part of Queen Regent Cersei Lannister's retinue when the queen does a circuit of the walls of King's Landing.[5]

Hallyne guides Tyrion Lannister through the Guildhall of the Alchemists explaining wildfire and the role of the Guild to him.[2]

During the preparations for the Battle of the Blackwater, Hallyne reports to Tyrion that the pyromancers have several thousands of jars of wildfire more prepared than expected, claiming that it partially is because of the discovery of a lost cache of Lord Rossart underneath the Dragonpit, and partially because the secret spells used to make the wildfire are working better than they had been doing before. He wonders aloud to Tyrion whether there are any dragons again, to explain for the improved efficiency of the spells.[3]

For services rendered during the Battle of the Blackwater, Hallyne is raised to a lordship, although no lands or incomes are given along with the title.[6]

A Feast for Crows

After the funeral for Lord Tywin Lannister, Lord Hallyne promises Cersei to put a flaming hand in the sky above King's Landing.[7] He and the other alchemists burn down the Tower of the Hand with wildfire under orders from Cersei after the wedding celebration of King Tommen I Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell.[8]

Cersei considers Hallyne as a candidate for the office of Hand of the King. Jaime Lannister tells her that Hallyne would be a terrible choice.[9]

After Dragonstone has been captured by the Iron Throne, Hallyne asks Cersei if the pyromancers might be allowed to hatch any dragon's eggs that might be found upon Dragonstone.[10]
