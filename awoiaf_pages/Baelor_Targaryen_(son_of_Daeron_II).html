Prince Baelor Targaryen, known as Baelor Breakspear, was a member of House Targaryen and the Prince of Dragonstone. He was the eldest son and heir of King Daeron II Targaryen and Queen Myriah Martell. Named after his grandfather's cousin, King Baelor I,[2] Prince Baelor served as his father's Hand of the King[1] after the Battle of the Redgrass Field until his death in 209 AC.[5]

In the television adaptation A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms, Baelor is portrayed by Bertie Carvel.

Appearance and Character

See also: Images of Baelor Targaryen

Prince Baelor had the dark hair of his mother, Myriah Martell, a Dornish princess. He kept it cut short, and by his late thirties, his hair was peppered with grey. He had a strong jaw which was clean-shaven. His nose had been broken twice. He was a tall,[1] lean, and broad-shouldered[7] man with long, straight legs and stood about a head taller than his youngest brother, Maekar. Although Baelor dressed simply, he had a weight about him that evoked a sense of power and certainty.[1]

A warrior, Baelor seemed to be all that could be wished in a knight, lord, or heir. He was a man who could win respect with ease, and he was as open-handed and just as his father, King Daeron II Targaryen.[5] He was one of the most powerful warriors in the Seven Kingdoms, rivalling the Kingsguard, and was famed for his prowess with sword and lance.[1] Baelor treated honorable foes with clemency.[2]

Many men looked upon Baelor's dark hair and eyes and muttered that he was more Martell than Targaryen.[5] Also, some Blackfyre sympathizers were bothered by his namesake, Baelor the Blessed, "the feeblest king who ever sat the Iron Throne".[2]

History

Early life

Prince Baelor was born in 170 AC,[4] the first child of King Daeron II Targaryen and Myriah Martell.[6] After the death of his grandfather, King Aegon IV Targaryen, in 184 AC,[8] Prince Baelor became the heir to the Iron Throne and was later given the title of Prince of Dragonstone by his father.[1]

Baelor learned how to fight under the Red Keep's master-at-arms, Ser Quentyn Ball, alongside his brothers and the Great Bastards.[9] In 187 AC, the seventeen-year-old Baelor won the name "Breakspear", following his famous victory at his aunt Daenerys's wedding tourney, where he defeated Ser Daemon Blackfyre, Daeron's bastard-born half-brother, in the final tilt.[5]

First Blackfyre Rebellion

Baelor arguing with his brother Maekar, by Even Mehl Amundsen ©

Baelor's dark hair and eyes led to wariness from those who disliked House Martell. Nobles from the Dornish Marches especially distrusted King Daeron and his heir, as the marcher lords were historical foes of Dornishmen and disliked their presence at court.[5]

Baelor distinguished himself in the First Blackfyre Rebellion by leading the Dornish and stormlands force which attacked the rearguard of Daemon's rebel army during the Battle of the Redgrass Field in 196 AC. Baelor's forces smashed the rebels against the shield-wall of his brother, Prince Maekar, inspiring a song about the hammer and the anvil.[2][9]

Hand of the King

Baelor was named Hand of the King and Protector of the Realm.[1] Ser Arlan of Pennytree had a high regard for Baelor, who was considered chivalrous and wise.[1][5]

When Prince Aegon Targaryen served at meetings of the small council, he observed that Baelor recommended clemency for honorable foes to prevent them from fighting to the death. In contrast, Brynden Rivers believed that pardoning rebels would only lead to future rebellions.[2]

Tourney at Ashford Meadow

Baelor by Lopata Four ©

In 209 AC, at the tourney at Ashford Meadow, Prince Baelor vouched for Ser Duncan the Tall because he remembered Duncan's old master, Ser Arlan of Pennytree. Baelor had jousted with Arlan in a tourney at Storm's End and had unhorsed him after breaking four lances. Due to Baelor's intervention, Duncan was allowed to enter the lists.[1]

When Duncan attacked Prince Aerion Brightfire to defend Tanselle, an innocent puppeteer, Baelor had a private word with Duncan and advised him. The next day Baelor sat together with Prince Maekar, Lord Leo Tyrell, and Lord Ashford in judgment of Duncan. Prince Aerion demanded a trial of seven, which was granted.[1]

With the help of his squire, Prince Aegon Targaryen, and Ser Raymun Fossoway, Duncan was able to recruit five men to fight for him. They needed one more, and because Baelor believed Duncan, he fought for him even though his nephew was the accuser.[1]

Before the melee began, Baelor advised Dunk's champions to use tourney lances, which would strike before their opponents' shorter war lances in the opening tilt. He also noted that the three knights of the Kingsguard on the opposing side would be sworn not to harm him, allowing him to safely engage them. Dunk's defending party won the trial, but Baelor had received a mortal blow to his head from the mace of his brother, Maekar, and the prince died in Dunk's arms. Baelor was cremated at Ashford, per House Targaryen funeral traditions.[1]

Recent Events

A Clash of Kings

Jeor Mormont, Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, briefly mentions Baelor's passing in a tourney mishap when explaining Maester Aemon's history to Jon Snow. Mormont tells Jon that Baelor left two sons,[10] Valarr and Matarys.[1]

A Storm of Swords

Maester Pylos includes Baelor when discussing historical Hands of the King with Lord Davos Seaworth, who has recently been chosen by Stannis Baratheon to serve as Hand. He tells Davos that the tenures of Hands of the King who were close kin were as varied as Baelor,[11] who was known to be a great Hand,[1] and Maegor Targaryen,[11] who was infamous.[12]

A Feast for Crows

Baelor's nephew, Maester Aemon, tells Samwell Tarly that his father never thought the throne would pass to him and often said it was punishment for the blow that killed his brother.[13]

The Winds of Winter

During the second siege of Meereen, when House Greyjoy's ironborn fleet comes ashore and begins to attack his Yunkish adversaries, Ser Barristan Selmy celebrates. He likens the arrival of a second force on their side to Baelor Breakspear and Prince Maekar, the hammer and the anvil.[14]

Family

Baelor married Jena Dondarrion, with whom he had two sons, Valarr and Matarys,[6] both of whom died in the Great Spring Sickness, no more than a year after their father's death.[2] Baelor's eldest brother, Aerys I Targaryen, succeeded their father Daeron, and was later succeeded by their youngest brother, Maekar I, who had dealt the blow that had killed Baelor.[15][16][2] According to the chronicles, Prince Maekar always bitterly regretted Baelor's death, and marked its anniversary every year.[5]

Baelor's son Valarr married Kiera of Tyrosh.[6] She gave birth twice, but both sons were stillborn.[9]
