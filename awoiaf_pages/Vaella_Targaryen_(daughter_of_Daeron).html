Princess Vaella Targaryen, called Vaella the Simple, was the daughter of Prince Daeron Targaryen and Kiera of Tyrosh.[1]

Character

Vaella was sweet but simple-minded.[2]

History

Vaella was born in 222 AC to Prince Daeron the Drunken, the eldest son of King Maekar I Targaryen,[2] and Kiera of Tyrosh, the widow of Prince Valarr Targaryen, Daeron's cousin.[1] Her father, then Prince of Dragonstone and Maekar's heir, died before his own father when Vaella was still young, leaving her as his only heir.[3]

When King Maekar died in 233 AC in battle against a rebel lord, a Great Council was called to settle the murky question of his successor. As the only child of Maekar's eldest son, Vaella's claim to the Iron Throne was considered, but dismissed immediately by the lords before any of the other candidates,[2] both on account of her mental capacity and gender. Ultimately, the Great Council instead chose Vaella's uncle, Maekar's youngest son Aegon, and declared him Aegon the Fifth of His Name.[3]

Recent Events

A Clash of Kings

Vaella and her mother Kiera, with Kiera's first husband Valarr Targaryen adorning the skies by coldraindropsss ©

When explaining the family history of the Night's Watch's Maester Aemon, an uncle of Vaella's, to his personal steward Jon Snow, Lord Commander Jeor Mormont told Snow of Vaella's father's death, her mental faculties, and how she was quickly passed over at the Great Council of 233 AC in favor of her other uncle, Aegon.[3]
