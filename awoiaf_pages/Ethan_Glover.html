Ethan Glover was a member of House Glover during the reign of King Aerys II Targaryen. He was the squire for Brandon Stark, the heir of Winterfell.[2]

History

When Brandon Stark learned of his sister Lyanna's abduction at the hands of Rhaegar Targaryen, Prince of Dragonstone, he took off for King's Landing with Jeffory Mallister, Kyle Royce, Elbert Arryn, and his squire, Ethan. When Brandon entered the Red Keep and demanded that Rhaegar come out and die, King Aerys II Targaryen had Brandon's party arrested for threatening the life of the crown prince. The fathers of Brandon and his companions were called to King's Landing to answer for the crimes of their sons. The king had the fathers and sons all executed, with only Ethan spared.[3] Aerys then demanded that Jon Arryn, Lord of the Eyrie, execute his wards, Robert Baratheon, Lord of Storm's End, and Brandon's younger brother Eddard Stark, now Lord of Winterfell. However, Jon refused the king's command, and Robert's Rebellion started shortly thereafter.[4]

After Aerys's death in the Sack of King's Landing, Eddard lifted the siege of Storm's End.[2] Lord Stark then rode to the Red Mountains with Ethan, Martyn Cassel, Theo Wull, Howland Reed, Ser Mark Ryswell, and Willam Dustin, Lord of Barrowton. At the tower of joy, they were confronted by the last three members of Aerys's Kingsguard, Ser Arthur Dayne, Ser Oswell Whent, and Lord Commander Gerold Hightower. All but Eddard and Howland were slain in the ensuing fight. Afterwards, Eddard had the tower pulled down, and used its stones for cairns for the fallen.[2]

Recent Events

A Game of Thrones

During a fever dream, Lord Eddard Stark recalls Ethan and his other former companions at the tower of joy.[2]

A Clash of Kings

Lady Catelyn Stark tells Ser Jaime Lannister that of Brandon Stark's companions who were arrested by King Aerys II Targaryen, Ethan was the only one to survive.[3]
