Ser Patrek of King's Mountain is a knight from King's Mountain, in the service of King Stannis Baratheon. He is one of the queen's men.[2]

Appearance

See also: Images of Patrek of King's Mountain

Patrek is clean-shaven, and windburnt.[3] The knight is bold and haughty.[3]

Patrek's cloth-of-silver surcoat is emblazoned with a blue five-pointed star.[3] He owns a cloak of white fur,[3] as well as a white wool cloak bordered in cloth-of-silver and patterned with five-pointed stars.[4][5]

Recent Events

A Dance with Dragons

Ser Patrek loses his lands in southern Westeros when King Stannis Baratheon sails for the Wall.[6] When Stannis marches for the battle beneath the Wall, Ser Patrek remains at Eastwatch-by-the-Sea with Queen Selyse Florent. Patrek accompanies her to Castle Black, is prepared to fight Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun, and escorts Selyse to the King's Tower.[3] He commands the queen's men protecting Selyse at the castle.[4]

Thinking he would have made a better husband for Alys Karstark than Sigorn, Patrek is angry during their wedding. He is hostile toward Satin, and has become smitten with Val.[6][4]

When Selyse decides that Patrek should marry Val, Jon Snow, Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, explains that free folk must be stolen, showing the man's strength.[5] Patrek is later slain at Hardin's Tower, where Val is staying, killed by Wun Wun. After tearing off the knight's sword arm, the giant smashes Patrek's body against the tower, ruining the knight's head. Jon attempts to calm Wun Wun, but the Lord Commander is then stabbed in the mutiny at Castle Black.[5]

Behind the Scenes

Patrek is named after Patrick St. Denis, a friend of George R. R. Martin and a resident of Montreal (which can be translated from French as Mount Royal or King's Mountain). St. Denis is a fan of the Dallas Cowboys American football team (whose logo is a blue star on silver), while Martin supports the rival New York Giants.[7]
