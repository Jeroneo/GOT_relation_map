Widow of the waterfront

Culture Volantene

Book A Dance with Dragons (appears)

The widow of the waterfront is a businesswoman and former bedslave who runs several docks, piers, and shipping lanes in a section of western Volantis. Her late husband was Vogarro, a triarch of Volantis.[1] Her given name is unknown.

Appearance and Character

See also: Images of Widow of the Waterfront

The elderly woman has wrinkled skin, a bent spine, and a hump on her back. Her white hair is so thin that the pink of her scalp shows through. The widow's eyes are bright and black. There are faint and faded scars on her cheek under her right eye, from where her tear tattoos were cut away after she was freed from slavery. She has a soft and sinister voice, and she speaks the Common Tongue with only a trace of accent.[1] The widow has a vulpine smile.[1]

The businesswoman's bodyguards are known as the widow's sons, and she has many captains in her debt.[1] Her customary table at the Merchant's House is well situated for security and privacy. Every petitioner must bring her a gift.[1][1]

The widow is opposed to slavery. She enjoys wine.[1]

The Old Blood of eastern Volantis refer to the widow as Vogarro's whore, though not to her face.[1]

History

The widow of the waterfront was once a pleasure slave taught the way of the seven sighs in Yunkai. She was purchased by Vogarro, a Triarch of Volantis. Vogarro fell in love with her, causing a great scandal, and an even greater scandal when he freed her and married her.[1]

Vogarro owned piers, storehouses, brokered cargoes, changed money, and insured shipowners against the hazards of the sea. When he died in 268 AC, his widow took over his business. She had to sell his manse, because no freedman may live within the Black Wall in eastern Volantis, regardless of wealth. She took up residence at the Merchant's House west of the Rhoyne, and set up her business in its common room.[1]

The widow once had a monkey which performed clever tricks.[1]

Recent Events

A Dance with Dragons

Gifts for the Widow, by Preston Stone © Fantasy Flight Games

The widow meets in the Merchant's House with Ser Jorah Mormont, who seeks passage in a ship to bring the captive Tyrion Lannister with him to Meereen to meet Daenerys Targaryen, Queen of Meereen. Jorah presents the widow with a pair of leather gloves he bought on the Long Bridge, which look cheap and tawdry next to the other gifts she had received that morning: a silver goblet, an ornate fan of thin translucent jade leaves, and an ancient bronze dagger marked with runes.[1]

The widow informs Jorah about the Golden Company preparing to sail for Westeros, and the exiled knight dispels rumors she has heard about Daenerys. She informs her guests about Volantene politics and the growing influence of Benerro, High Priest of the Temple of the Lord of Light.[1]

The widow is initially reluctant to aid the Westerosi due to Jorah's attitude and because she does not trust his motivations in seeking Daenerys. She begins to relent after Tyrion speaks honestly about his intentions, but their conversation is interrupted when Tyrion is attacked by Penny over the death of her brother, Oppo. After the dwarfs are separated, the widow has the inn's landlord bring Penny up to her rooms and given wine and new clothes to wear.[1]

The widow decides that Volantis is not safe for dwarfs, and she tells the travelers to take the Selaesori Qhoran, which is leaving for Qarth in two days. Since Jorah is trying to get to Meereen instead of Qarth, the widow explains that Benerro has had a fire vision that the ship will not make it to its ultimate destination. On behalf of the slaves in Volantis, she asks the Westerosi to tell Daenerys that the slaves of Volantis are waiting for her and that she should come soon.[1]

The widow writes a binder for the mates of the Selaesori Qhoran, all free men, promising to pay their ransom in case of capture.[2] The ship, crippled by a storm while sailing on the Summer Sea toward Qarth, is eventually boarded by slavers.[3] While the mates are ransomed, the slave sailors and the Westerosi are sold into slavery in a Yunkish camp near Meereen.[2]
