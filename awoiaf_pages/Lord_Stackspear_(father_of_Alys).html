Lord Stackspear (father of Alys)

From A Wiki of Ice and Fire
Jump to: navigation, search
Note: The canonicity of the character presented in this article is currently unclear. They are not mentioned in the published works of George R. R. Martin. However, they are mentioned in an expanded version of the Westerlands chapter from The World of Ice and Fire that was published on Martin's website a few months before the book release date.[1] Until further information comes out, they should be considered a semi-canon character.
Lord Stackspear
Title Lord
Allegiance House Stackspear
Culture Westermen
Issue Alys Stackspear

Lord Stackspear was the head of House Stackspear during the reign of King Aegon V Targaryen.

History

Lord Stackspear's daughter, Alys Stackspear, was hastly married to the fifteen-year-old Jason Lannister after he took her maidenhood and got her pregnant. The marriage was short lived as Alys died giving birth to their son, Damon Lannister.[1]

During the misrule of the westerlands by Lord Tytos Lannister, Lord Stackspear doubled the taxes on his smallfolk. Ignoring his liege lord's protests, he hired a company of Volantene sellswords to enforce his extortion.[1]

Lord Stackspear and his levies were later part of the host of Lord Tytos's son, Ser Tywin Lannister, when he suppressed the Reyne-Tarbeck revolt in 261 AC.[1]

Family

Lord Stackspear had one daughter, Alys Stackspear. His relation to the current head of House Stackspear, Lord Selmond, is unknown. He may have been Selmond's grandfather.
