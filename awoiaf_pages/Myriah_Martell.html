Princess Myriah Nymeros Martell was a Dornish princess, and the wife and queen consort of King Daeron II Targaryen.

Appearance

See also: Images of Myriah Martell

Myriah had the typical dark hair and eyes of the members of House Martell. Those traits were passed on to her eldest son, Prince Baelor Targaryen.[5][1]

History

Myriah was the eldest daughter of the Prince of Dorne during the reign of the Young Dragon, King Daeron I Targaryen.[2] She had a younger brother, Prince Maron Martell.[1]

In 161 AC, after Daeron was killed during his attempted conquest of Dorne, King Baelor I Targaryen freed the Dornish hostages in King's Landing and returned them to Dorne personally, walking barefoot. Once he reached Sunspear, Baelor spoke with the Prince of Dorne, and agreed upon a peace, which included the betrothal of Myriah to Baelor's cousin, Prince Daeron Targaryen. As both Daeron and Myriah were children at the time, the two were to marry once they were both of age.[2]

Princess Myriah eventually wed Prince Daeron, and their marriage was happy and fruitful.[1] They would have four sons together: Baelor (born 170 AC), Aerys (born 172–177 AC), Rhaegel (born 173–178 AC), and Maekar (born 174–179 AC).[6] Myriah's marriage to Daeron would pave the way for Dorne to come under the rule of the Iron Throne.[1]

In 184 AC, after the death of King Aegon IV Targaryen, Daeron succeeded him as King Daeron II Targaryen, and Myriah became queen.[1] Many noble Dornishmen and women flocked to Daeron's court, which served to give life in the Red Keep a foreign flavor; however, this alienated many of Daeron's other nobles and courtiers. Some also looked upon Prince Baelor's dark hair and eyes and muttered he was more Martell than Targaryen. These seeds of dissent would eventually grow into the First Blackfyre Rebellion.[1]

It is unknown whether Myriah survived her husband, who died of the Great Spring Sickness in 209 AC.[7][1]

Behind the Scenes

In A Song of Ice and Fire, the character was originally named Myriah Martell.[8][9] But when writing The World of Ice & Fire, George R. R. Martin changed her name to "Mariah".[2][6] However, when writing Fire & Blood, George changed her name back to the original spelling, "Myriah".[10] The latest version of The World of Ice & Fire has been edited to change her name to "Myriah".[11]
